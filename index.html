The Process

Your eyes are
the keyboard.

01

Camera Tracks Your Gaze

A standard front-facing camera — or an external eye-tracking device — reads your eye position in real time with precision calibration.

02

Gaze Lands on a Key

The on-screen keyboard highlights the key your eyes are fixed on. A short dwell time (customizable) confirms the selection — no click needed.

03

Words Are Built in Real Time

Predictive text and phrase suggestions reduce effort — users type full sentences using minimal eye movements.

04

Speech Output (Optional)

Tanaw can read composed text aloud via text-to-speech, enabling verbal communication in social or clinical settings.

Capabilities

Built for
real needs.

Tanaw is designed in close collaboration with clinicians and occupational therapists to ensure it meets the nuanced needs of patients with ALS, cerebral palsy, spinal cord injuries, locked-in syndrome, and more.

Precision Eye Tracking

Works with standard webcams and dedicated eye-tracking hardware. Sub-degree accuracy ensures confident key selection.

Smart Word Prediction

AI-powered suggestions learn your communication patterns to reduce keystrokes and speed up expression over time.

Custom Phrase Banks

Caregivers and clinicians can pre-load common phrases for fast access — ideal for urgent needs or daily routines.

Text-to-Speech Output

High-quality voice synthesis reads composed messages aloud in real time — multiple voice options and languages.

Adjustable Interface

Key size, dwell time, color contrast, and layout can all be tuned by a clinician to match each patient's capability.

Privacy-First Design

All processing happens on-device. No sensitive health or communication data is sent to external servers.

Patients & Caregivers

Who Tanaw
helps.

Neurological

ALS & Motor Neuron Disease

As voluntary muscle control declines, Tanaw preserves the ability to communicate — giving patients independence for as long as possible.

Developmental

Cerebral Palsy

Individuals with limited limb control can use gaze to compose messages, make choices, and engage in education and social life.

Acquired Injury

Spinal Cord Injury

Patients with high-level cervical injuries retain full cognitive function but lack motor ability — Tanaw gives them a voice without physical effort.

Intensive Care

Locked-In Syndrome

For patients with complete or near-complete paralysis, Tanaw may be the primary or only means of expressive communication.
