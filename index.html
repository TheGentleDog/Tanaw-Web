The Process

Your eyes are
the keyboard.

01

Caregiver Sets Up the Device

A caregiver or clinician installs Tanaw and calibrates the camera to the patient's eye position. Setup takes only a few minutes and is done once.

02

Camera Tracks the Patient's Gaze

MediaPipe Iris detects facial landmarks and tracks iris position in real time via a standard webcam. A Kalman Filter smooths gaze data to reduce jitter — no specialized hardware needed.

03

Words Are Suggested Instantly

The N-gram Candidate Module predicts full words from abbreviated or partial inputs using Damerau-Levenshtein Distance — so typing "mbhy" can resolve to "mabuhay" in one gaze.

04

Speech Output (Optional)

Tanaw can read composed text aloud via text-to-speech, enabling verbal communication in social or clinical settings.

Capabilities

Built for
real needs.

Tanaw is designed with clinicians and occupational therapists to meet the nuanced needs of patients with ALS, cerebral palsy, spinal cord injuries, locked-in syndrome, and more.

Precision Eye Tracking

Powered by MediaPipe Iris and a Kalman Filter for real-time, noise-resistant gaze estimation — using any standard built-in or external webcam. No specialized hardware required.

N-gram Word Prediction

The N-gram model is trained on corpora generated by RoBERTa-Tagalog and RoBERTa-Base — combined with Damerau-Levenshtein Distance to predict and correct words from partial or abbreviated inputs, achieving ~97% suggestion accuracy in both English and Filipino.

Auto-Saved Phrase Shortcuts

When a patient types the same sentence 3–4 times, the system automatically saves it as a predefined phrase. One gaze on the Predefined Sentence button brings up the full list — no retyping needed.

Text-to-Speech Output

High-quality voice synthesis reads composed messages aloud — supporting both English and Filipino for real-life communication.

Caregiver-Friendly Setup

Initial calibration is handled by a caregiver or clinician. Once set up, the patient operates the full system independently through gaze alone.

Privacy-First Design

All processing happens on-device. No sensitive health or communication data is sent to external servers.

Patients & Caregivers

Who Tanaw
helps.

Neurological

ALS & Motor Neuron Disease

As voluntary muscle control declines, Tanaw preserves the ability to communicate — giving patients independence for as long as possible.

Developmental

Cerebral Palsy

Individuals with limited limb control can use gaze to compose messages, make choices, and engage in education and social life.

Acquired Injury

Spinal Cord Injury

Patients with high-level cervical injuries retain full cognitive function but lack motor ability — Tanaw gives them a voice without physical effort.

Intensive Care

Locked-In Syndrome

For patients with complete or near-complete paralysis, Tanaw may be the primary or only means of expressive communication.
